## Road to Budapest — A Narrative

### September 2025 — The League Phase Begins

Arsenal kicked off their third consecutive Champions League campaign in the new expanded format — 36 clubs, 8 matches in a single league table. The pressure was immense: two consecutive semi-final exits (one of which was against the eventual winner PSG in 2024/25) had sharpened expectations.

Viktor Gyökeres, arriving from Sporting CP wearing the number once belonging to Thierry Henry, scored twice in the opening match. The message was clear: the striker question had finally been answered.

Eight matches. Eight results without defeat. 21 goals. Bayern Munich were beaten 3–1 at the Emirates in November — Arsenal's most emphatic home European victory in years. By January, when the league phase concluded, Arsenal had topped the table and earned a favourable path through the bracket.

---

### February 2026 — Round of 16: Leverkusen

Bayer Leverkusen — the German champions of 2023/24, one of Europe's most physically complete sides — were the round of 16 opponents. The first leg in Germany ended 1–1 after a composed Arsenal performance. Ødegaard and Rice dominated the midfield battle. Gyökeres troubled their back line throughout.

At the Emirates three weeks later, Arsenal were ruthless. Gyökeres opened the scoring in the 22nd minute. A second came before half-time. 2–0. 3–1 on aggregate. Into the quarter-finals — where they had exited in 2023/24, a memory that burned.

---

### March 2026 — Quarter-Final: Back to Leverkusen

Wait — there was no quarter-final against Leverkusen. Arsenal's round of 16 opponents were Leverkusen (confirmed above). The quarter-final bracket drew Arsenal against **Inter Milan**.

Inter — six-time European champions, finalists in 2024/25 — were formidable. Arsenal won both legs, 2–0 at the Emirates and 1–0 in Milan. Saliba and Gabriel were imperious. Raya made two crucial saves in the San Siro. The 14-match unbeaten record in European competition was growing.

---

### May 2026 — Semi-Final: Atlético Madrid

Diego Simeone's Atlético were everything Arsenal fans dreaded: brutal in their organisation, lethal on the break, capable of making any game ugly. The first leg at the Metropolitano ended 1–1 — a fair result with both sides creating chances.

The second leg at the Emirates was electric. Saka — Arsenal's irreplaceable No. 7 — scored on 44 minutes, drifting inside from the right and finishing into the far corner. It was the sort of goal only he could score. Arsenal defended the lead with grit and intelligence for 46 minutes. When the whistle blew, the Emirates erupted.

> *"We have waited so long for a night like this. We go to Budapest."* — Mikel Arteta
