## Arsenal's Tactical Identity in Europe

### The Base System: 4-3-3 (Possession) / 4-2-3-1 (Out of Possession)

Arteta's Arsenal operate in two distinct structures depending on whether they have the ball.

**In possession:**

```
         Gyökeres
   Martinelli   Saka
     Ødegaard
  Rice   Zubimendi
Timber  Gabriel  Saliba  White
            Raya
```

**Out of possession:**

```
      Gyökeres   Saka
        Ødegaard
     Rice     Zubimendi
 Timber  Gabriel  Saliba  White
              Raya
```

The transition between these two shapes — almost instantaneous in Arsenal's best performances — is what makes them so hard to counter.

---

## Key Tactical Principles

### 1. Inverted Full-Backs

Arsenal's full-backs — White on the right, Timber or Zinchenko on the left — tuck inside when Arsenal have possession, creating a midfield 5 that overwhelms opposition pressing structures. This allows Saka and Martinelli to operate in 1v1 situations wider, while the inverted full-backs become passing options between the lines.

This was the defining tactical innovation of Arteta's early years, pioneered with Zinchenko and refined with Timber and White.

---

### 2. Rice as the Progressive Carrier

The evolution of Declan Rice from West Ham's shield midfielder to Arsenal's most dynamic box-to-box player is a coaching masterclass. Arteta has given Rice licence to drive forward from central midfield — often 30–40 yards — creating numerical overloads in advanced zones. His 10 goals in 2025/26 reflect this freedom.

In the UCL, Rice's forward carrying against Leverkusen was the decisive tactical edge in the round of 16.

---

### 3. High Line, High Press

Arsenal defend from the front. Gyökeres leads a high press that denies opponents the time to play out from the back. The defensive line — Saliba and Gabriel — holds a high position, compressing the pitch vertically and forcing opposition play into channels where Rice and Ødegaard can win second balls.

The risk: pace in behind. The management: Saliba's recovery speed is elite, and Raya's sweeper-keeper positioning covers the space.

---

### 4. Set-Piece Efficiency

Arsenal scored 18 Premier League goals from set-pieces in 2025/26 — the highest in the division. Ødegaard's delivery, Gabriel's aerial presence, and Merino's late runs from the second post are a lethal combination. In the UCL, four goals came from set-piece situations in the knockout rounds.

---

### 5. Controlled Aggression in Away Legs

Arsenal's record in away UCL knockout matches in 2025/26:
- At Leverkusen: 1–1 (controlled)
- In Madrid (semi): 1–1 (absorbed pressure, counter-attacked)

The template is clear: don't concede, use transitions, and take your moments. Gyökeres and Saka are the perfect pair to execute this — explosive, direct, decisive.
