## 2025/26 UEFA Champions League — Arsenal's Campaign

Arsenal's 2025/26 Champions League campaign was historic in almost every positive sense — right up until the very last kick in Budapest. They became only the fourth team in UCL history to reach the final while remaining unbeaten across 90 minutes in every game. They lost the title on penalties.

---

## League Phase — Unbeaten in Eight

Arsenal entered the new expanded league phase — 36 clubs, 8 matches each — and topped their section without defeat. Highlights included:

| Match | Result |
|---|---|
| Arsenal vs Bayern Munich | **3–1** (Emirates) |
| Club Brugge vs Arsenal | 0–3 (away) |
| Arsenal vs Atlético Madrid | 2–1 (Emirates) |

They remained unbeaten across all eight league phase games, scoring 21 goals and conceding just 5 — the best attacking record of any side in the first round.

### Semi-Final — Atlético Madrid

| Leg | Result |
|---|---|
| Leg 1 (away) | 1–1 |
| Leg 2 (home) | 1–0 |
| **Aggregate** | **2–1** |

Saka's 44th-minute strike at the Emirates was enough to send Arsenal to Budapest. It was the club's 14th consecutive UCL match without defeat — the longest unbeaten run in the competition's knockout history.

---

### The Final — Paris Saint-Germain (Budapest, 30 May 2026)

**Arsenal 1–1 PSG (PSG win 4–3 on penalties)**

Venue: Puskás Aréna, Budapest, Hungary

| Event | Time | Player |
|---|---|---|
| Goal: Arsenal | 5' | Kai Havertz |
| Goal: PSG (pen) | 67' | Ousmane Dembélé |
| Full time | 90' | 1–1 |
| Extra time | — | No further goals |
| Penalty shootout | — | PSG win 4–3 |

Havertz struck early, giving Arsenal the dream start. PSG responded through a Dembélé penalty after a controversial handball decision. The game remained level through 90 minutes and extra time, leaving the final to be decided by penalties — where the Gunners fell at the last, as PSG defended their Champions League title.

<Warning>
  Arsenal were the better team for large portions of the final. The penalty shootout outcome does not reflect the balance of a game in which the Gunners had more possession, more shots, and more expected goals. The margin between heartbreak and history could not have been thinner.
</Warning>
