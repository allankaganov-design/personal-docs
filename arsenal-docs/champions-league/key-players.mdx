## The Players Who Will Decide It

Squads win league titles. Individuals win European trophies. When the margin in a knockout match comes down to one pass, one save, or one moment of courage, these are the Arsenal players whose greatness will be required.

---

## #7 — Bukayo Saka

<Info>
  Saka is the player. When he is on form — running at defenders, drawing fouls, cutting inside to deliver or shoot — Arsenal are categorically more dangerous than any opponent.
</Info>

In the 2025/26 UCL campaign, Saka created the most chances of any player in the knockout rounds. His 44th-minute goal against Atlético Madrid in the semi-final second leg was the moment that made the final possible.

**What is required in 2026/27:** Stay fit. His workload across a Premier League season and UCL campaign is enormous. Managing his minutes in domestic football to preserve peak intensity for European knockout nights is Arteta's most important selection decision.

---

## #41 — Declan Rice

Rice is the engine. His ability to do both defensive and offensive work at elite level — winning the ball, immediately progressing it, arriving late into the penalty area — gives Arsenal an extra attacker from deep that opponents cannot track without exposing themselves elsewhere.

**What is required in 2026/27:** More goals from outside the box in UCL matches. Rice's technique and power make him capable of a 20-goal season if his shooting platform from advanced positions is maintained.

---

## #8 — Martin Ødegaard

The captain sets the tempo. When Ødegaard is finding pockets, rotating the ball quickly, and delivering in dangerous areas from set-pieces, Arsenal's possession becomes suffocating. When he is off form or physically below-par, Arsenal become predictable.

**What is required in 2026/27:** Avoid the injury interruptions that cost him 8 games in 2025/26. When fit, he is irreplaceable.

---

## #14 — Viktor Gyökeres

Seven UCL goals in a debut season. Gyökeres answers the question every Arsenal fan has had since Thierry Henry retired: what does it look like when Arsenal have a world-class No. 9?

**What is required in 2026/27:** Maintain the output, improve the link-up play in tight spaces against deep blocks. PSG's midfield physically outmuscled him in stretches of the final. Strength and physicality work in preseason will be essential.

---

## #2 — William Saliba

Saliba rarely makes headlines because he rarely makes mistakes. His positioning, reading of the game, and pace give Arsenal a near-unbreachable defensive baseline. Without him, Arsenal do not reach the final.

**What is required in 2026/27:** Become the first name on the Champions League Team of the Tournament. It is well within reach.

---

## #1 — David Raya

Raya was excellent throughout the campaign. But in the shootout in Budapest, Arsenal needed more. Kepa — widely regarded as one of the best penalty-stopping goalkeepers in the world — did not take over.

**What is required in 2026/27:** A clearly defined plan for penalties. Whether that means Raya works with a specialist to improve his shootout record, or Arteta designates Kepa as the shootout goalkeeper, the decision needs to be made, drilled, and settled before the first UCL game in September.

---

## #22 — Ethan Nwaneri (Returning)

The wildcard. Nwaneri returns from his loan at Marseille — where he is expected to have had 30+ senior starts — as one of the most technically gifted 19-year-olds in Europe. He may not start every UCL game. But as a second-half substitute in a tight semifinal or final, his combination of creativity, pace, and unpredictability could be the difference.

**What is required in 2026/27:** Arrive back at Arsenal ready to compete for a starting spot. Let Arteta decide when to use him in Europe. When called upon, deliver.

---

<Note>
  The through-line across all seven players is the same: staying fit, staying focused, and performing at their absolute best in the 5–6 games that decide the Champions League from February onwards. Everything before Christmas in the league phase is preparation for those nights.
</Note>
