## The Founding Era — 1886 to 1925

### Dial Square, 1886

Arsenal Football Club was founded in **1886** by workers at the Royal Arsenal munitions factory in Woolwich, south-east London — first under the name **Dial Square**, a nod to the sundial above the entrance to the main workshop. Within weeks the club was renamed **Royal Arsenal**, and the workers' team began playing informal matches on Plumstead Common.

What began as a recreational outlet for factory hands became something altogether more serious. By 1891 Arsenal had turned professional, and in **1893** they became the **first southern club to be elected to the Football League** — a historic milestone that set the club apart from every other London institution at the time.

---

### The Move to Highbury — 1913

The most consequential decision in Arsenal's early history came in 1913, when chairman Sir Henry Norris orchestrated the club's move from Woolwich to **Highbury, Islington** in north London. The relocation was controversial — Arsenal were relegated shortly after and had to fight their way back to the First Division. Norris secured their re-election to the top flight in 1919 through political maneuvering that remains debated to this day.

The move north proved transformative. Highbury became one of English football's most famous and beautiful grounds — and Arsenal became a north London institution, setting up the rivalry with Tottenham Hotspur that endures over a century later.

---

### Herbert Chapman and the Birth of Modern Arsenal — 1925

The arrival of manager **Herbert Chapman** in 1925 is the true beginning of Arsenal as a trophy-winning club. Chapman introduced revolutionary tactical thinking — most notably the **WM formation** that redefined English football — and turned Arsenal into the dominant force of the 1930s.

<Tip>
  Arsenal's famous red-and-white kit — with the distinctive red body and white sleeves — was introduced under Chapman. The white sleeves were added so Chapman could spot his players more easily from the dugout.
</Tip>

Under Chapman (and his successor George Allison after Chapman's sudden death in 1934), Arsenal won:

- **5 First Division titles** (1930/31, 1932/33, 1933/34, 1934/35, 1937/38)
- **2 FA Cups** (1930, 1936)

This was the first great Arsenal dynasty — a blueprint the club has spent 90 years trying to replicate.
