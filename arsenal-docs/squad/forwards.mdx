## Forwards

The arrival of Viktor Gyökeres transformed Arsenal from a team that lacked a genuine elite centre-forward into one that can — and did — trouble any defensive line in Europe. Paired with Bukayo Saka (the world's best wide forward in 2025/26 by most metrics), Arsenal's attack was both prolific and relentless.

---

### Strikers

  <Card title="#14 — Viktor Gyökeres" icon="futbol">
    **Nationality:** Swedish

    Gyökeres arrived from Sporting CP for ~£63.5m wearing the No. 14 shirt famously worn by Thierry Henry — a statement of intent from the club. He delivered: 28 goals and 9 assists across all competitions in 2025/26, including the opener in the Champions League quarter-final. His pressing intensity and hold-up play are as important as his goals.

    Jesus's role has evolved from starter to influential rotational option following Gyökeres's arrival. His high-pressing intensity, link-up play, and ability to play across the front three make him a valuable tactical tool rather than a passenger.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#29 — Kai Havertz" icon="futbol">

    Havertz scored the opening goal of the 2025/26 Champions League final — a fifth-minute strike in Budapest — before Arsenal ultimately lost on penalties. His hybrid striker-midfielder profile is one Arteta uses strategically in possession-dominant game plans. 14 goal contributions across the season.

### Wingers

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="#7 — Bukayo Saka" icon="star">
    **Nationality:** English  
    **Age:** 23  
    **Status:** First choice, right wing  
    **Weekly Wage:** £300,000 (highest earner)  

    Saka is Arsenal's talisman and the best winger in world football by most assessments. His 4-year extension until 2030 — making him the club's highest earner at £300,000 per week — was the most important contract in English football this season. 22 goals and 17 assists across all competitions. Won the decisive penalty in the Champions League semi-final against Atlético Madrid.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#11 — Gabriel Martinelli" icon="futbol">
    **Nationality:** Brazilian  
    **Age:** 24  
    **Status:** First choice, left wing  

    Martinelli's pace, directness, and relentless pressing make him the perfect counterpart to Saka on the opposite flank. He netted 14 times in all competitions and formed a devastating left-side partnership with Timber and Merino.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#20 — Noni Madueke" icon="futbol">
    **Nationality:** English  
    **Age:** 23  
    **Status:** Rotation, right wing  

    Signed from Chelsea for ~£40m, Madueke gives Arteta an alternative profile on the right — a more direct, explosive dribbler compared to Saka's intelligence and technique. His full impact will be seen in 2026/27 after a settling-in season.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#19 — Leandro Trossard" icon="futbol">
    **Nationality:** Belgian  
    **Age:** 30  
    **Status:** Rotation, left wing / false 9  

    Trossard remains one of the most versatile attackers in the Premier League. He contributes from multiple positions and is particularly dangerous off the bench. Signed a financially improved contract extension this season.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#22 — Ethan Nwaneri" icon="futbol">
    **Nationality:** English  
    **Age:** 18  
    **Status:** Emerging / Development  

    Nwaneri swapped his academy shirt for No. 22 after a breakout campaign, and he's widely regarded as one of the most gifted teenagers in European football. His technical quality and composure far exceed his years. On loan at Marseille for 2025/26 to gain regular starts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
