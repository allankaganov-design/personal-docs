## Arsenal FC — 2025/26 Wage Bill

<Info>
  Arsenal's estimated gross fixed salary total for 2025/26 is **£185.7 million per year** (£3.57 million per week), based on Capology figures. Including performance bonuses and signing fees, total compensation obligations exceed £230 million annually — the second-highest in the Premier League behind Manchester City.
</Info>

---

## Context: Revenue vs. Wage Spend

Arsenal's record revenue of **£691 million** in fiscal year 2024/25 — driven by the club's return to Champions League football, kit partnerships, and commercial growth — provided the headroom to push wages to this level sustainably.

| Metric | Figure |
|---|---|
| Annual revenue (2024/25) | £691 million |
| Gross wage bill (2025/26) | ~£185.7 million |
| Wage-to-revenue ratio | ~26.8% |
| Premier League ranking | 2nd (behind Man City) |
| Average first-team weekly wage | ~£132,600 |

A wage-to-revenue ratio under 30% — at a club spending at this level — is considered financially healthy. Arsenal's commercial growth has allowed significant player investment without breaching FFP thresholds.

## Wage Bands

Arsenal's structure concentrates spend at the top, with a clear drop-off between the elite earners (Saka, Havertz, Saliba) and the mid-tier signings. This "top-heavy" approach rewards match-winning quality while keeping the broader squad wage bill manageable.
