## Arsenal's Investment Model

Arsenal's financial model has undergone a complete transformation since the post-Wenger transitional years. The club is now a self-sustaining top-four institution with the commercial footprint and ownership commitment to fund a Champions League-winning squad.

---

## Revenue Pillars

Arsenal's record **£691 million revenue** in 2024/25 is generated across five streams:

| Revenue Stream | Estimated Contribution |
|---|---|
| Broadcast (PL + UCL) | ~£280 million |
| Commercial & Sponsorship | ~£220 million |
| Matchday (Emirates Stadium) | ~£110 million |
| Player trading (net) | ~£50 million |
| Other (licensing, etc.) | ~£31 million |

---

## The Emirates Stadium Advantage

Emirates Stadium — capacity 60,704 — is the fifth-largest ground in England and generates the highest matchday revenue of any non-expanded English stadium. The club's long-term plan to expand the stadium to approximately 67,000 seats would add an estimated £25–30 million in annual matchday income.

---

## KSE's Role

Kroenke Sports & Entertainment (KSE), led by Stan and Josh Kroenke, acquired 100% of Arsenal Holdings in 2018. While KSE has historically avoided direct equity injections — preferring self-funded growth — the arrival of Andrea Berta as sporting director in 2025 and the club's summer spend signal a shift in posture: KSE is willing to underwrite short-term deficit spending in pursuit of the Champions League.

<Note>
  Unlike City Group, Red Bull, or Chelsea's ownership model, Arsenal's investment approach is revenue-first — building the commercial base before increasing player expenditure. This is slower but produces more sustainable competitive advantage.
</Note>

## The Transfer Philosophy Under Berta

New sporting director Andrea Berta (formerly of Atletico Madrid) brought a distinctive transfer philosophy:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify Early">
    Target high-ceiling players 1–2 seasons before they peak in market value (e.g., Mosquera, Lewis-Skelly).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Loan-to-Buy Structures">
    Use loan arrangements with purchase options (e.g., Hincapié) to spread costs across financial years.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sell Decisively">
    Move players on at peak value rather than letting contracts run down — generating accounting profit and freeing wages.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reinvest in the Core">
    Concentrate spend on the 6–8 players who start the biggest games, while keeping squad depth relatively lean.
  </Step>
</Steps>
