## Midfielders

Arsenal's midfield depth is arguably the most complete of any English club, blending creative excellence, physical dominance, and technical quality across every position. The summer addition of Martin Zubimendi — the best defensive midfielder in La Liga — solved a long-standing rotation concern behind Declan Rice.

---

### Central / Defensive Midfielders

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="#41 — Declan Rice" icon="arrows-split-up-and-left">
    **Nationality:** English  
    **Age:** 26  
    **Status:** First choice, defensive/box-to-box mid  
    **Weekly Wage:** £225,000  

    Rice has grown into the complete midfielder since his £105m arrival from West Ham. His engine, defensive coverage, progressive carrying, and goal contributions (10 goals across all competitions in 2025/26) make him Arsenal's most important non-goalkeeper. He was central to the UCL run and wore the captain's armband on several occasions.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#36 — Martin Zubimendi" icon="arrows-split-up-and-left">
    **Nationality:** Spanish  
    **Age:** 26  
    **Status:** First choice rotation, defensive mid  

    Signed from Real Sociedad — where he wore #36 and began his professional career — Zubimendi brings elite positional intelligence and clean ball-winning to give Rice genuine rest. His arrival filled the last structural gap in Arteta's midfield blueprint.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#16 — Christian Nørgaard" icon="arrows-split-up-and-left">
    **Nationality:** Danish  
    **Age:** 30  
    **Status:** Rotation  

    The Danish international, signed from Brentford, is a tenacious and experienced option who provides a different profile to Rice and Zubimendi — more combative and direct. Excellent in transitions and tight game management.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Attacking / Creative Midfielders

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="#8 — Martin Ødegaard (Captain)" icon="star">
    **Nationality:** Norwegian  
    **Age:** 27  
    **Status:** First choice, No. 10 / right half-space  
    **Weekly Wage:** £240,000  

    Arsenal's captain and creative heartbeat. Ødegaard's vision, link-up play, and set-piece delivery are irreplaceable. He averaged 8.2 progressive passes per 90 minutes in the Premier League this season — the most of any player in the top five leagues. His partnership with Rice is the best central midfield pair in England.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#23 — Mikel Merino" icon="arrows-split-up-and-left">
    **Nationality:** Spanish  
    **Age:** 28  
    **Status:** First choice, left/central mid  
    **Weekly Wage:** £130,000  

    Merino has become one of Arteta's most trusted starters, offering a physical yet technical profile from the left side of midfield. His goal-scoring ability from range — and aerial threat at set-pieces — gives Arsenal a different dimension, particularly in big European games.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#28 — Albert Sambi Lokonga" icon="arrows-split-up-and-left">
    **Nationality:** Belgian  
    **Age:** 25  
    **Status:** Squad / Cup rotation  

    Lokonga provides experienced depth and appeared in domestic cup fixtures throughout the season. His future at the club beyond 2025/26 remains subject to summer discussions.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  In the 2025/26 Champions League, Arsenal's midfield — principally Rice and Ødegaard — averaged 65% possession across all 14 matches. Their control was a defining feature of the Gunners' run to the final.
</Note>
