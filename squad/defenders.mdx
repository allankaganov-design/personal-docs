## Defenders

Arsenal's defensive line is the foundation on which Arteta's system is built. The first-choice back four of Saliba, Gabriel, White, and an attacking full-back (Timber or Zinchenko) conceded only 28 Premier League goals in 2025/26 — the fewest of any title winner since the Invincibles.

---

### Centre-Backs

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="#2 — William Saliba" icon="shield-halved">
    **Nationality:** French  
    **Age:** 24  
    **Status:** First choice, right CB  

    Widely regarded as one of the top two centre-backs in world football, Saliba's combination of pace, composure, aerial dominance, and range of passing makes him irreplaceable. He signed a 5-year extension in 2025 (£250,000/week) and is Arsenal's defensive talisman heading into the Arteta era's defining years.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#6 — Gabriel Magalhães" icon="shield-halved">
    **Nationality:** Brazilian  
    **Age:** 27  
    **Status:** First choice, left CB  

    Gabriel's physicality, leadership, and penalty-box goalscoring (he weighs in with 8–12 goals per season from set-pieces) make him the perfect foil for Saliba. The Brazilian has become a cult hero at the Emirates and committed to a 4-year deal until 2029 (£200,000/week).
  </Card>

  <Card title="#3 — Cristhian Mosquera" icon="shield">
    **Nationality:** Colombian  
    **Age:** 21  
    **Status:** Rotation / Cover  

    Signed from Valencia for ~£30m, Mosquera impressed with his composure and ability to play out from the back. He made 18 appearances in all competitions, providing reliable cover and growing into Premier League football ahead of a bigger role in 2026/27.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#33 — Riccardo Calafiori" icon="shield">
    **Nationality:** Italian  
    **Age:** 22  
    **Status:** Rotation / Hybrid CB-LB  

    Calafiori's versatility — equally comfortable as a centre-back or left-back — gives Arteta tactical flexibility. His progressive carrying from deep was a key weapon in Arsenal's build-up play during the UCL campaign.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Full-Backs

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="#4 — Ben White" icon="shield">
    **Nationality:** English  
    **Age:** 27  
    **Status:** First choice, right back  

    White's inverted right-back role — tucking inside to overload the midfield — is central to how Arsenal maintain width through Saka while controlling central zones. One of the Premier League's most underrated technical defenders.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#12 — Jurrien Timber" icon="shield">
    **Nationality:** Dutch  
    **Age:** 23  
    **Status:** First choice, left back / right back  

    After his injury-interrupted debut season, Timber has become a crucial multipurpose defender — equally effective on either flank. His energy, quality on the ball, and defensive intelligence give Arteta several tactical options in a single player.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#17 — Oleksandr Zinchenko" icon="shield">
    **Nationality:** Ukrainian  
    **Age:** 28  
    **Status:** Rotation, left back  

    The original architect of Arsenal's inverted full-back system, Zinchenko remains an important option — especially in matches where Arteta wants more midfield control via his left-back position.
  </Card>

  <Card title="#49 — Myles Lewis-Skelly" icon="shield">
    **Nationality:** English  
    **Age:** 19  
    **Status:** Emerging starter, left back  

    One of the most exciting young defenders in Europe, Lewis-Skelly broke into the first team this season with performances that belied his age. He is expected to challenge Timber for a regular starting berth in 2026/27.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
