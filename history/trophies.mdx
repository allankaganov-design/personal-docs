## Arsenal FC — Honours Record

Arsenal are the most successful football club in London, with a trophy cabinet spanning nearly 140 years of competition.

---

### FA Cup

Arsenal hold the all-time record for FA Cup wins with **14 titles**:

1930, 1936, 1950, 1971, 1979, 1993, 1998, 2002, 2003, 2005, 2014, 2015, 2017, 2020

<Note>
  Arsène Wenger's seven FA Cups (1998, 2002, 2003, 2005, 2014, 2015, 2017) made him the most successful manager in the competition's history.
</Note>

### League Cup (EFL Cup)

Arsenal have won the League Cup **twice**: 1987 and 1993.

## European

### UEFA Cup Winners' Cup
- **1993/94** — Beat Parma 1–0 in the final in Copenhagen. George Graham's final European night.

### UEFA Champions League
- **2005/06 Final** — Reached the final for the first time, losing 2–1 to Barcelona in Paris. Jens Lehmann was controversially sent off after 18 minutes.
- **2025/26 Final** — Reached the final for the second time, losing to PSG 1–1 on penalties in Budapest.

<Warning>
  Arsenal have never won the Champions League or its predecessor the European Cup. Ending that story is the defining mission of the Arteta project.
</Warning>

### UEFA Fairs Cup
- **1969/70** — Beat Anderlecht 4–3 on aggregate. Arsenal's first major European trophy.

---

## Community Shield

Arsenal have won the FA Community Shield (and its predecessor the Charity Shield) **16 times** — the joint most in the competition's history.

---

## Women's Trophies (Selected)

Arsenal Women are one of England's most decorated women's clubs:

- **UEFA Women's Champions League**: 2007, **2025** (2024/25 season)
- **Women's Super League**: Multiple titles
- **Women's FA Cup**: 14 titles

<Tip>
  Arsenal Women's 2024/25 Champions League victory — their second European title, and first in 18 years — came weeks before the men's team reached their second-ever UCL final. Both clubs reached the summit of European football in the same calendar year.
</Tip>
