## Top Earners — 2025/26

Arsenal's highest-paid players are a deliberate list: every player at the top of the wage table is either a current match-winner or a cornerstone of the club's long-term planning. There are no legacy contracts propping up declining contributors.

---

## #1 — Bukayo Saka — £300,000/week

Saka's new 4-year deal until 2030 made him the highest-paid player in Arsenal's history and the most important contract renewal in English football in 2025. The decision to make him the outright highest earner — above Havertz, Jesus, and even Ødegaard — signals that Arsenal view Saka as their generational superstar for the next five years.

At 23, Saka is already one of the most complete wide forwards on the planet: 22 goals, 17 assists across all competitions in 2025/26. The contract extension eliminates any near-term risk of Premier League or European rivals poaching him.

---

## #2 — Kai Havertz — £275,000/week

The German forward is Arsenal's second-highest earner — a source of some debate given the arrival of Gyökeres — but Havertz's versatility and his goal in the Champions League final have cemented his value to Arteta's system. He gives the squad a genuine elite false-9 option, a profile no other Arsenal player replicates.

---

## #3 — Gabriel Jesus — £265,000/week

Jesus's wage reflects the market rate at which he was signed from Manchester City, rather than his current hierarchical standing at the club. Following Gyökeres's arrival, he has effectively become a high-quality rotation option on a top-earner contract — a structural tension the club will need to address in the coming transfer window.

---

## #4 — Martin Ødegaard — £240,000/week

Ødegaard and Rice share an identical wage packet — a symbolic decision that underscores their equal standing as the club's two most important players after Saka. The captain earns every pound: his creativity, professionalism, and leadership have been central to the Premier League and Champions League runs simultaneously.

---

## #5 — Declan Rice — £225,000/week

Rice's salary, set at the time of his £105m transfer from West Ham, ranks fifth but remains excellent value given his evolution into a complete midfielder under Arteta. His 10 goals and 7 assists in 2025/26 made him one of the best midfielders in Europe on a per-game basis.
