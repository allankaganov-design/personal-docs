## Financial Fair Play & PSR Compliance

Arsenal's aggressive spending in the summer of 2025 — nearly £200 million on incoming transfers — was made possible by a combination of record revenue, disciplined outgoings, and the financial architecture built under the ownership of KSE (Kroenke Sports & Entertainment).

---

## UEFA Financial Sustainability Regulations (FSR)

UEFA's Financial Sustainability Regulations replaced FFP in 2023. The key threshold under FSR is a permissible deficit of **€60 million over a rolling three-year period** (rising to €90m if backed by owner equity injections).

Arsenal's Champions League qualification in 2023/24, 2024/25, and 2025/26 has generated:

| Season | UCL Revenue (est.) |
|---|---|
| 2023/24 (QF exit) | ~£75 million |
| 2024/25 (SF exit) | ~£95 million |
| 2025/26 (Final) | ~£120 million |

This three-year UCL revenue stream — combined with Premier League domestic income and commercial growth — means Arsenal operate within UEFA's thresholds comfortably, even at a £185m+ wage bill.

---

## Premier League PSR (Profit & Sustainability Rules)

Under PSR, clubs must not exceed a £105 million loss over a rolling three-year window. Arsenal's strategy to remain compliant includes:

- **Strategic player sales** — Outgoing deals for players not in Arteta's plans (e.g., Jorginho to Flamengo, Kieran Tierney departures) generate accounting profit.
- **Amortisation management** — Long-term contracts spread transfer fee costs over contract duration.
- **Revenue growth** — The club's commercial partnerships and stadium expansion plans are structured to grow the revenue base ahead of wage increases.

<Warning>
  Arsenal's summer 2025 spend of ~£200m in fees is near the outer limit of what PSR allows without triggering a risk of breach. The club's financial team has publicly committed to maintaining a margin of safety. Future transfer activity will be partly contingent on successful player sales.
</Warning>

---

## The FFP-Busting Masterplan

Arsenal's reported "£171m FFP-busting masterplan" heading into summer 2025 was built around the assumption that Champions League final prize money (approximately £90–100m), new commercial deals, and Hincapié's loan-to-buy structure being treated as an installment sale would provide the necessary accounting relief.

The club went on to reach the final — confirming the financial planning was sound.

<Info>
  Premier League champions receive no additional prize money beyond the standard broadcasting-split model, though Champions League qualification and performance prize money is transformative. Arsenal's UCL final appearance generated an estimated **£120 million in prize money, TV distributions, and matchday revenue** in 2025/26 alone.
</Info>
